About Me

My passion for computing lies at the intersection of human-computer interaction (HCI) and artificial intelligence. During my undergraduate studies, I recognized how intuitive design impacts human well-being, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic. This led me to pursue research projects involving user perception in VR environments and cognitive load in human-AI interaction.

As a Ph.D. student at RIT's CLaSP Lab and an AWARE-AI trainee, I aim to improve how people interact with intelligent systems. My goal is to create accessible, user-centered technology that benefits fields like education, healthcare, and decision-making.
